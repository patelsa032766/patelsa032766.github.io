Grammar Cards
Verb stem + はじめる · beginning an action
読みはじめました
Attaches to the verb stem to express starting an action. Here はな starts reading — marking the turning point of the story.
〜ようになる · coming to do / reaching a state
その本を読むようになりました
Expresses a gradual change into a new habit or state. Used here to show that reading became part of Hana's life.

Your path forward

JLPT N4 to N1 —
a clear progression

KanjiAI is built around structured vocabulary progression, giving you an objective measure of where you stand on Japan's standard language proficiency scale — independent of any single course or textbook.

N4
JLPT N4 — Elementary
~1,500 words. Daily life, work, travel, basic opinions. You can have simple conversations and understand slow, clear speech. This is where structured study begins to pay off.
→ Sections 1–4 of your course, reinforced by KanjiAI's SRS review
N3
JLPT N3 — Intermediate
~3,750 words. Newspapers, manga, longer conversations. The inflection point where Japanese starts to feel readable rather than decoded.
→ Sections 5–6 + Enhanced 10K expansion in KanjiAI
N2
JLPT N2 — Upper Intermediate
~6,000 words. Business Japanese, news media, academic texts. Understood without needing context from the surrounding situation.
→ Top 10K expansion carries you through N2 vocabulary territory
N1
JLPT N1 — Advanced
~10,000 words. Nuanced, complex Japanese across all genres. Native-level comprehension of written and spoken material.
→ The full Top 10K expansion targets this vocabulary range
Why grammar matters at every level

Vocabulary gets you to N4. Grammar gets you to N1. KanjiAI's reading passages expose you to real grammar constructs — te-form connections, conditional patterns, causative-passive structures — in sentences built from words you already know. Understanding how the language fits together is what separates recognition from fluency.
